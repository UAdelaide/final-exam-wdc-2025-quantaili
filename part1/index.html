Dog Facts

Dogs have been companions to humans for over 14,000 years. They are descendants of wolves and have evolved to be one of the most diverse species on the planet. There are over 340 recognized dog breeds worldwide, each with unique characteristics, temperaments, and physical attributes. Dogs have an exceptional sense of smell, up to 100,000 times more sensitive than humans. They can detect certain cancers, predict seizures, and find missing persons. The smallest dog breed is the Chihuahua, while the largest is the Great Dane. Dogs can understand up to 250 words and gestures, count up to five, and perform simple mathematical calculations. The average dog has the intelligence equivalent to a two-year-old human child.
